My role

I was apart of the mobile payment team and designed products on both the consumer and maintenance side of the marketplace. I collaborated with engineers to create the HankookNFC 'payapp', Japan service of payapp 'paytomo' experience.

What is Payapp?

The Payapp service allows you to make payments offline and to make a payment with SMS or Kakao to remote customers, regardless payment methods and payment environments, by installing apps on your smartphone. This is a new concept payment solution that allows you to receive payments easily.

UX objectives

While the Engineering teams addressed the common concerns shared by merchants (i.e. security, technology, and costs), I focused on improving the user experience for both merchants and customers by:

  • 1. Optimize checkout flows to make transactions faster for customers, yet accommodate the needs of retailers.
  • 2. Design promotion materials that could be applied quickly to different merchants and communicate important messages, transaction information, and prompts to customers.

    • Personar

    Following my initial research, I held user interviews with both small business owner and servers. Based on this research, I had a deeper understanding of the common problems that needed to be solved. I took these common problems and created several personas in order to successfully market to our customers. It was essential to understand their mindset, preferences, motivations, behaviors, and goals to be successful.

    Homepage

    Our team chose to redesign company homepage because of its confusing user flow and visual design. In addition, we wanted to challenge ourselves to simplify a multi-functional website to provide essential information for a first time user wanting to learn about HankookNFC.

    My main role in this project was to develop the front-end framework and design how it would be responsive across multiple touchpoints. In this, I considered what information would be more suitable on a desktop experience versus a mobile experience, while still maintaining fluidity through desktop resizing.     • Conclusion

    Our customers include small business owners, servers, business man, and students etc. To understand all of our users and how our products fit into their daily lives, we would constantly immerse ourselves in the places where our customers work. We needed to go to them and watch how they use the product we’ve designed.

    I worked closely with developers to define the necessary KPI’s. Before releasing any new features, we made sure to list metrics we wanted to focus on for that specific feature. The purpose here is to learn as you go, then reinvest this information to make better product decisions.
