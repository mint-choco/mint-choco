HankookNFC

Fintech Startup

May 2017 - June, 2018

Sketch, Html, CSS, Javascript, After Effect

My role

I was apart of the mobile payment team and designed products on both the consumer and maintenance side of the marketplace. I collaborated with engineers & pm's to create the HankookNFC 'payapp', Japan service of payapp 'paytomo' experience.

What is Payapp?

The Payapp service allows you to make payments offline and to make a payment with SMS or Kakao to remote customers, regardless payment methods and payment environments, by installing apps on your smartphone. This is a new concept payment solution that allows you to receive payments easily.

Design Principles

It was a bit tempting to just jump right into sketch and push pixels for hours given the time constraint. However, in order to best design a consumer facing app like this one, I believe it is important to first understand

  • 1. Who am I designing for?
  • 2. What kinds of problems do they currently face?
  • 3. How can this app fit into the desired product-market?

    • Research

    Research helps find answers and I can start to brainstorm in the back of my head. I conducted my research online, discussing with colleagues, and looking at competitive apps in this space.

    From talking with my colleagues and seeing the experience of current Fintech apps, it just made sense that Square was the clear solution for one reason: speed. It’s quick, convenient, and secure. I needed to prioritize speed as my primary design principle and reinforce simplicity into the design.

    Logo

    Homepage

    I began to structure out what the most important content would be for display: Snappywire's overview, contact information, services etc. The website is a visually modern update to the current site.

    Application

    I identified the key specs that had to be included in the app in order to build the MVP(minimum viable product), then mapped out user flows. At this point in the process, I found participatory design to be extremely helpful. I actually had end users mapping out their ideal flows, which I was then able to incorporate into my design.

    Information Architecture

    Flow Map

    Based on the user flows, I constructed the information architecture such that there was a minimal level of difficulty to immediately accessing relevant information. After all, the goal of this product is to enable users to quickly and easily sell items.

    Low-Fidelity Prototyping

    Based on the main features and task flow of platform, I created initial sketches & wireframes of prototype with the following user flows: register items, payment request, receipt, mypage etc feature. I used Sketch as the tool for Low-Fidelity prototyping the app.

    User Interface

    I designed the interface to be clean and simple, devoid of any distractions in order to maximize the user’s focus while paying. A palette of blues and whites also served to calm the user and keep them focused.
